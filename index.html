    PROBLEM SPACE

    Why Inference Is Unverifiable

    01

    Hardware Entropy

    IEEE-754 execution diverges across heterogeneous accelerators and CPU microcode. The same model, different silicon — different floats.

    02

    Silent Kernel Drift

    Attention and fused kernels introduce non-canonical float paths under parallel execution. Drift is silent, cumulative, and undetectable without a commitment scheme.

    03

    Unverifiable Inference

    Non-deterministic logits invalidate artifact commitments and downstream proofs. You cannot audit what you cannot reproduce.

    SOLUTION

    What PCMP Does

    ⊕

    Canonicalize

    Platform-Canonical Merkle Protocol

    Maps every hardware-specific float byte-stream to a single canonical representation. M4_MAX_HASH == NVIDIA_H100_HASH after canonicalization.

    CORE PRIMITIVE
    ⬡

    Commit

    Merkle Anchoring

    Produce a deterministic Merkle root over the canonical artifact. Immutable. Auditable. Committable to any ledger or verification system.

    MERKLE ROOT
    ↩

    Reconstruct

    Zero-Overhead Reversibility

    Verify the commitment, then restore the original hardware-specific byte stream without padding, approximation, or information loss.

    LOSSLESS
    ZERO-OVERHEAD REVERSIBILITY

    Commit Without Losing Fidelity

    Canonical form is an intermediate, not a replacement. The original byte-stream survives intact. Proof and precision coexist.

    PROTOCOL

    Verification Steps

    01
    Ingest raw logit stream from hardware Native IEEE-754 bytes, no preprocessing.
    02
    Apply PCMP Canonicalization Normalize float representation to canonical form. pcmp.canonicalize(stream)
    03
    Compute Merkle Root Deterministic tree over canonical chunks. Root is portable across all hardware.
    04
    Anchor commitment Publish root to ledger, chain, or verification record.
    05
    Reconstruct original stream Reverse canonicalization losslessly. pcmp.restore(canonical) → original bytes.
